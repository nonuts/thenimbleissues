The Art Of: Mike Miller aka mikedia

Sometimes you have to force yourself into a rhythm to get things done, and to improve your work.

Mike Miller has done exactly that: he painted a piece of artwork each day, for 12 months.

A daily routine

"It was a personal challenge to stop being complacent and get more artwork done. I'd been dormant most of 2011, and before that was lucky to get one piece done a week. A daily routine seemed to be the best way to prevent procrastination, and I made sure to publicly acknowledge it so I wouldn't get lazy or give up."

If you want to see all the artwork Mike has done, check out his flickr-set 365 days of art.

Mike goes on answering The 5 Nimble Q's :

Why? "It's fun, it makes me a happier person. Getting lost on a canvas helps me reflect and let go of the little things that I shouldn't get so hung up on."

Where? "Mostly at home, my fortress of solitude :)"

How? "Using an iPad3 (or whatever they called it) and Procreate. I also picked up a Pogo Connect after reading the Nimble Mag article on it, and 95% love it. Life is a lot easier when you don't have to fake pressure sensitivity. The 5% is for when it confuses my palm with the stylus, which doesn't happen often, but it really hampers workflow when it does."

When? "Whenever I can, except for early mornings. My brain refuses to be creative before 8am."

Tips? "For pen/illustration brushes, I like to increase the max-size to get more variation in strokes, and then zoom in on the canvas for better control. Controlled variation I guess? It gives me a happy middle ground where I have detail, but not too fine of detail where things get washed out."

Midday-Moon

Being a highly skilled web-developer as well, Mike even did a web-comic during his art-marathon, using all kinds of nifty web-technology and of course, it was painted on the iPad.
