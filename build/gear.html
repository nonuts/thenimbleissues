Setting it up

The Pogo Connect doesn't have an on/off button. It runs on a regular AAA battery which supplies the device enough power to last for weeks. I had mine on for over a month now and used it on a daily basis, and it's still at 75%. So that's cool. It also means the connect has a slightly thicker body to fit the battery in which gives it an overall feeling of a crayon.

Different to other bluetooth devices, you don't need to connect or register the pogo through the according system preferences first. You can simply connect from within the apps and you're good to go.

Adjusting the apps

In most apps, the pressure you put on the pogos tip is mapped to either shape or opacity dynamics (or both) which again you can set within the app itself. It needs some fiddling here and there to get the effect you want, but overall it's fairly easy to get used to. Only Paper by 53 comes with a fixed default, which works nice as well.

So once you got that under control, you're good to go. The pogo connect works great for all kind of sketches and drawings. The pressure sensitivity is very gradually distributed, and the device needs quite a firm grip for good results. Compared to the Adonit Jot Touch it really feels more like a crayon, while the Jot feels more like a brush.

But what's with fingerpainting?

We often like to kinda develop a Stockholm Syndrome with the constraints we embrace when doing something new. Maybe call it purism. So the question is: will your art get better using a pressure sensitive stylus instead of a finger? Not necessarily, even though I think most sketches will profit from it. What's really great though is that it breaks your flow less. You can now not only control the thickness of your stroke without losing focus, but also undo your actions using the small button on the connect. I'm positive future stylus will have have more interaction options built in like that, like we already can see with Wacom stylus.

It's obvious the pogo connect so far got implemented in painting and note taking apps first; I wonder if devices like that will eventually find their way into sound and instrumental apps as well. At least a theremin should be nicely doable with it.

Conclusion

Overall I really like the device. If I had to criticize anything I would say it lacks good grip. Since you have you apply more pressure compared to the jot, I often accidentally pressed the button on the pogo. Eventually I held it a little more towards the top and used the app undo button. And even though I got to really like the precision disc of the jot, the wider rubber tip of the connect didn’t really degrade the overall experience for me.
